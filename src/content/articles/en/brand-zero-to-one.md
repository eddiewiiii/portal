October 2022, Guangzhou. A desk in a co-working space, rent just over a thousand a month, the surface just big enough for a laptop and a cup. Outside the window were the towers of Tianhe, and in the afternoons the sun came in at an angle and left a bright strip along the corner of the desk. This was the first prep meeting I called for Penguin Exploration, three of us counting me.

The co-working space always had people moving through. The coffee machine was going, someone was on a call, chair legs scraped the floor, the sounds all mixing together. I turned the laptop toward the two across from me, on the screen the activity framework I had built in Notion, page linking to page, like a net. One of them was a teacher who had run study tours before, she looked at it and said, the framework is clear enough, but for the first event you have to decide who you're taking, where, and to do what. I nodded, palms a little damp.

First days are always like this. You have an idea in your hands, but the idea is soft, blurry, something you can't even explain clearly to yourself, and you have to make it into a place other people want to walk into. The step from zero to one, you can call it a few nodes, but living through it is just your palms sweating the whole way.

April 2025, Changsha. Different city, different desk, this time at a new company. The company had just rented a floor, the desks were new, the chair had a catch when you spun it. The job I took on was to build a brand system in thirty days.

I opened Notion at my desk and started making documents. The first was called Brand Positioning, the second Visual System, the third Content Framework, one after another, and by the time I was past a dozen the links between documents began to hold together. The thing about Notion is you can build it like blocks, break a concept into pieces and put it back. I dragged things around on the screen, moved one block, the whole structure shifted with it.

Those weeks I often stayed late rewriting copy. Spring in Changsha is damp, the workstation got cold at night, I draped my jacket over the back of the chair. Rewriting copy on a screen is as slow as on paper, sometimes I'd get stuck half an hour on a single title. But with Notion, once I saved, everyone saw the newest version, no sending files, no version-matching. One night I was at it until two in the morning, the elevator had stopped, I took the stairs down, and in the stairwell the sound-controlled lights came on one by one and went off one by one.

Halfway through, I looked back at those documents and saw they had grown from a pile of scattered ideas into a place a person could walk into. Positioning was the doorway, and moving inward the walls and lighting belonged to the visual system, and further in the content framework set out what was placed in the rooms. The step from zero to one is turning the blurry into the clear, gathering the scattered, so that the person passing by wants to stop and step in.

December 2025, still Changsha. Puzi Bar, I had become a co-founder. My partner's surname was Zhou, a few years older than me, he had been in food and beverage for over a decade. We sketched the first version of the menu on a cocktail napkin, Brother Zhou's handwriting was better than mine. That stretch of road had a lot of foot traffic at night, outside the window people lined up for milk tea, the sound-controlled light kept going on.

Puzi went through those same nodes again from zero to one. The first day of each node, my palms sweated. It was the same at that co-working desk for Penguin Exploration, and the same at the new company's workstation.

That day Brother Zhou asked me, why are you nervous every time you start. I said I didn't know, maybe I'm afraid what I make won't be a place anyone wants to walk into. He laughed, said nothing, wiped the cup and set it down.

The line outside had broken up, the milk tea shop had closed. Another sound-controlled light went off.
