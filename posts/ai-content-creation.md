有一天晚上，我用AI工具列了三十多个选题方向。

其中有一个看起来特别完美，标题对仗、角度新颖、逻辑自洽，甚至连开头都替我想好了。我盯着它看了五分钟，总觉得哪里不对。说不出来哪里不对，但就是不想写。

最后还是弃了。

用AI工具辅助做选题优化差不多一年了。这一年的感受可以用两个字概括：分裂。一方面我确实已经离不开它，另一方面我又时刻在提醒自己不要被它驯化。

过去做一个深度内容的选题，我可能需要花一整个上午去发散、查资料、筛角度，再和人讨论。现在我用AI做第一步的发散，同一时间能得到几十个备选方向，然后再花主要精力做判断、做深度加工。这种工作流的改变，不是更快地写完一篇文章，而是把人的判断力前置了，让模型负责信息的广度，我来负责洞察的深度。

但问题也随之而来。选题优化被极度提效之后，内容生产的温度变得特别容易流失。AI生成的建议通常逻辑正确、结构工整、看起来什么都说了，但就是缺一种人味，那种基于个人经历的不完美视角，或者某种只有特定语境下才会出现的表达方式。这种温度一旦被抹平，内容就会变成流水线上的标准件，读者是能感觉出来的。

扔掉那个完美选题的那天晚上，我想明白了一件事：AI给我的方向越完美，我越要警惕。因为完美的东西往往是平均的，而真正好的选题，一定带着某个人的偏执和盲区。

这半年我一直在找一个平衡点。有几个做法我觉得比较有效。

AI是找选题的陪练，不是定选题的老师。我会大量地让它列出方向，但最终选哪个，一定是基于我自己的经验、判断，甚至是一瞬间的直觉。就像那天晚上那三十多个方向，AI列得很好，但真正想写的那个，是第二天早上洗澡的时候突然冒出来的。

AI生成的文字我会读，但不会直接贴。我会重新用自己说话的方式去写一遍，哪怕结构没那么规整。

有时候我会故意给自己制造一些摩擦。完全抛开AI，用最笨的办法去翻书、去手写大纲，只为了保持那种把脑袋想疼的感觉。这听起来低效，却是在保护创作中人的温度。

《品牌相关性》里其实也提到，人们之所以和某些品牌产生强烈连接，往往不是因为它们更正确，而是因为它们更像一个人。内容创作也是这样。AI可以让我们变得更高效、更周全，但它不能替代那个我为什么这么想的动机，也不能替代那些不完美但真挚的表达。

现在我更愿意把AI工具当成一个放大器，而不是替代品。它让我有更多时间去做真正重要的事：观察、思考、感受，然后把那些只属于人的温度放进每一个选题里。

那天晚上扔掉的那个选题，后来在另一个语境下，变成了你现在读到的这篇。

One night, I used an AI tool to generate thirty-something topic ideas.

One of them looked perfect,balanced title, fresh angle, airtight logic, even the opening paragraph was drafted for me. I stared at it for five minutes. Something felt off. I couldn't say what exactly, but I just didn't want to write it.

In the end, I threw it out.

I've been using AI tools to help with topic brainstorming for about a year now. The feeling, if I had to compress it into one word, is split. On one hand, I genuinely can't work without it anymore. On the other, I'm constantly reminding myself not to get domesticated by it.

It used to take me a whole morning to develop a deep-dive topic,brainstorming, researching, filtering angles, discussing with someone. Now I let AI handle the first pass of divergence. In the same amount of time, I get dozens of candidate directions, then spend my real energy on judgment and deep processing. This shift in workflow isn't writing articles faster,it front-loads human judgment. The model covers breadth; I cover depth.

But the problem followed close behind. Once topic brainstorming got hyper-efficient, the warmth of content production became very easy to lose. AI-generated suggestions are usually logically correct, structurally neat, and look like they've said everything,but they're missing a kind of human feel. The imperfect perspective born of personal experience. The expression that only surfaces in a specific context. Once that warmth gets smoothed out, content turns into an assembly-line standard part, and readers can tell.

The night I threw out that perfect topic, something clicked: the more perfect the direction AI gives me, the more I need to be on guard. Because perfect things tend to be average. And genuinely good topics always carry someone's particular obsession and blind spot.

I've been looking for a balance point over the past six months. A few things have worked.

AI is a sparring partner for finding topics, not a teacher who decides them. I let it generate a lot of directions, but which one I actually pick is always based on my own experience, judgment, or even a split-second instinct. Like that night,those thirty-something directions were good, but the one I actually wanted to write came to me the next morning in the shower.

I read AI-generated text, but I don't paste it in. I rewrite it in my own speaking voice, even if the structure ends up less clean.

Sometimes I deliberately create friction for myself. I set AI aside entirely, go back to the dumbest method,flipping through books, handwriting outlines,just to keep that feeling of making my head hurt. It sounds inefficient. It's protecting the human warmth in the work.

Brand Relevance actually makes a similar point: people form strong connections with certain brands not because they're more correct, but because they're more like a person. Content creation works the same way. AI can make us more efficient, more thorough,but it can't replace the motive behind why I think this way, and it can't replace those imperfect but sincere expressions.

These days, I prefer to think of AI tools as an amplifier, not a replacement. It gives me more time for what actually matters: observing, thinking, feeling,and putting the warmth that only belongs to humans into every topic.

That topic I threw out that night,it eventually became, in another context, the piece you're reading right now.
