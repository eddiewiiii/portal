2017年那个夏天，香港尖沙咀一栋写字楼的二十几层，冷气开得让人想加件外套。我坐在长桌一头，对面是亚洲万里通的人。亚洲万里通，国泰航空的里程计划，那年我们跟它谈租租车的合作。冷气风口正对着我的后颈，我把杯子往旁边挪了挪。

会议桌上摆着两台笔记本电脑，一台是我带来的，一台是他们的。我打开方案，讲到一半，对面那个戴眼镜的女生点了一下头，说这个积分兑换的入口，我们可以做首个同类型平台合作伙伴。我手心有点潮，但没让它看出来。会议室的玻璃墙外面是维多利亚港，船在海上慢慢走。

我那时候已经在租租车做了几年，主要管品牌和合作。租租车做出境自驾，把国内的人送到国外的车里，再把国外的路开一遍。说到底就是把人从一个地方挪到另一个地方，中间那段挪动的过程，是我们要填的缝。亚洲万里通把人从地面挪到天上，里程积分在中间架一座桥。两个生意看起来差很远，拆到最底层，都是把人从一个场景送进另一个场景。

冷气还在吹，我喝了一口水，水是凉的。

2020年，我换了地方，广州TIT创意园。易车的广州分部在那儿。园区里有几家咖啡馆，下午总有人端着杯子走来走去。我的工位靠窗，能看见楼下绿化带的几棵树。

我在那儿做MO卡，车主会员体系。易车做汽车媒体和交易很多年，手里有海量的车主数据，但这些人散在各个页面、各个App里，没有一个统一的地方让他们停一停。MO卡想做的就是把这些人拢起来，给他们一个能站住脚的身份。跟租租车把出境自驾的人拢到一起，是一回事。人是同一个人，从看车的页面走到会员卡页面，从研究去哪自驾走到下单租车，中间要跨好几道坎。

那时候我常加班。园区晚上九点以后基本没人了，只有几栋楼的灯还亮着。我在工位上对着屏幕改方案，窗外能听见园区里保洁阿姨拖地的声音。我把MO卡的权益拆了又拆，拆到最小颗粒，再拼回去。拆拼的过程里我慢慢想清楚一件事：跨行业做品牌，能带走的东西就那么几样，其中一样是把人从一个场景送到另一个场景的能力。

2023年。广州南沙，一个农业公园类型的农场。企鹅探学，我后来自己做的研学品牌，那天是第一场活动。好几十组家庭报名，比我预想的多。农场里有一片菜地，绿油油的，孩子们蹲在地里拔萝卜、摘青菜，家长在旁边拍照。有个小男孩蹲在地里不起来，他妈叫他，他不肯走，手里攥着一把小白菜。

我站在菜地边上看着。那天下午太阳不算太毒，但蹲久了腿会麻。有个妈妈走过来问我，这些菜能不能带回去吃。我说能。她拎着一袋菜走了，孩子在后面跑着追她。

研学这个词说起来大，拆开看就是把城里的孩子送到田里，把田里的东西带回家。

冷气回来了，我又坐回尖沙咀那间会议室。杯子里的水凉了，对面的女生合上电脑，说那我们推进一下。我点了一下头。冷气从头顶的风口吹下来，吹在后颈上。

Summer 2017, a tower in Tsim Sha Tsui, Hong Kong, somewhere above the twentieth floor. The air conditioning was set low enough that I wanted a jacket. I sat at one end of a long table, and across from me sat the people from Asia Miles. Asia Miles, Cathay Pacific's mileage program, and that year we were negotiating a partnership with them for Zuzuche. The vent was blowing straight onto the back of my neck. I moved my cup to the side.

Two laptops on the table, mine and theirs. I opened the deck, got halfway through, and the woman with glasses across the table nodded and said, on this points-redemption entry, we can do it as the first platform partner of this kind. My palms were a little damp, but I kept that to myself. Beyond the glass wall of the meeting room was Victoria Harbour, boats crawling slowly across the water.

I had been at Zuzuche a few years by then, handling brand and partnerships. Zuzuche did overseas self-drive rentals, took people from China and put them into cars abroad, and had them drive foreign roads. Stripped down, it was moving people from one place to another, and the moving itself was the seam we had to fill. Asia Miles moved people from the ground to the sky, miles and points building a bridge in between. The two businesses looked far apart, but pulled apart to the bottom, both were about carrying a person from one scene into another.

The air kept blowing. I drank some water, it was cold.

2020, a different place, Guangzhou, TIT Creative Park. Yiche's Guangzhou office sat there. The park had a few coffee shops, and in the afternoons people wandered around holding cups. My desk was by the window, I could see a few trees in the green strip below.

I was building MO Card there, the car-owner membership system. Yiche had been in auto media and transactions for years, holding a huge amount of owner data, but these people were scattered across pages and apps, with nowhere unified for them to stand still. MO Card was meant to gather them, give them an identity that could hold its weight. Saying it out loud, it was the same thing as Zuzuche gathering people who wanted to drive overseas. Same person, walking from a car-browsing page to a membership card page, from researching where to drive to placing a rental order, crossing several thresholds in between.

I worked late a lot then. After nine the park was mostly empty, lights on in only a few buildings. I sat at my desk revising the deck, and outside the window I could hear the cleaning auntie mopping the floor. I broke MO Card's benefits down, then down again, to the smallest grain, then built them back up. Somewhere in that taking apart and putting back, a thing clarified for me: doing brand across industries, the things you can carry with you are few, and one of them is the ability to move a person from one scene into another.

2023. Nansha, Guangzhou. A farm, the kind they call an agricultural park. Penguin Exploration, the study-tour brand I later ran myself, and that day was the first event. Dozens of families signed up, more than I expected. The farm had a vegetable plot, bright green, and kids squatted in the dirt pulling radishes and picking greens, parents photographing them from the side. One small boy refused to stand up, his mother called him, he wouldn't come, his fist full of baby bok choy.

I stood at the edge of the plot and watched. The sun that afternoon wasn't too harsh, but squatting long enough made your legs numb. A mother walked over and asked me, can we take these vegetables home to eat. I said yes. She walked off carrying a bag of greens, her kid running behind her trying to catch up.

Study tour sounds like a big word, pulled apart it's just sending city kids to a field and bringing the field back home.

The cold air came back, I was sitting in that Tsim Sha Tsui meeting room again. The water in the cup had gone cold, the woman across from me closed her laptop and said, let's push this forward. I nodded. The air conditioning blew down from the vent overhead, onto the back of my neck.
