2023年冬天，广州番禺，一个政企项目的竞标现场。会议室不大，圆桌，几把椅子，墙上挂着一块白板，白板上还留着上午别的会写的字，没人擦。我坐在桌子这边，对面坐着几个甲方的代表，其中一个中年男人听了一会儿，打了个哈欠，没捂嘴。

我手里攥着一份方案，A4纸，二十几页。我讲到第八页的时候，他打哈欠。我停了两秒，看他，他没看我，在看窗外。那天广州很热，会议室空调开了，但吹不到我这边，我后背有点潮。

研学这行从2022年开始就卷得厉害。我做企鹅探学，最早一场活动是2023年，在广州南沙，一个农业公园类型的农场。那天好几十组家庭来，农场里有一片菜地，孩子们蹲在地里摘菜，有个小男孩蹲着不肯起来，手里攥着一把青菜要带回家吃。我站在菜地边上看着，太阳不算太毒，但蹲久了腿麻。那场办下来我觉得这事儿能做，家长愿意来，孩子愿意蹲下去。

没过多久就变了。

家长群里的比价越来越狠。一条线路，五个机构报五个价，差几十块家长就能聊一个下午。有人把五家的行程表拼到一张图里发到群里，逐项比对，几点集合、几点吃饭、有没有含保险、几点送回。家长比得比采购还细。

线路同质化也压上来。广州做研学的，五家里有四家做同一条非遗线路，同一个传承人，同一个作坊，孩子们带回去的手工作品都一样。我那年去看了一圈，回来在共享空间的桌子上坐了很久，没说话。对面工位的人问我怎么了，我说没事，在想怎么跟别人不一样。

2024年春天，香港科技大学广州校区那场活动算是个例外。我设计了一条跟校园科研结合的线路，把孩子们带进实验室外面那条走廊，让他们看研究生在做什么。那场转化率做到60%，自然流量增长40%。但那是一场一场磨出来的，磨的时候手心一直在出汗。

后来竞标现场那个打哈欠的中年男人，最后选了另一家。那家报价比我低，线路跟我差不多。我走出会议室的时候，走廊的声控灯亮了一下，又灭了。我站在那儿等它再亮，它没亮。我跺了一下脚，它亮了。

卷这个字，说到底就是大家挤同一个地方。要破这个局，得找到别人没蹲过的那块地。南边那个农场的小孩攥着青菜不肯走，那一下我记了很久。家长愿意为那种蹲下去的瞬间付钱，不愿意为一张跟别人一样的行程表付钱。

回到家里，我翻开一个旧文件袋，封面上的照片是2023年那场农场活动，几个孩子蹲在地里，背对着镜头，手里攥着菜。窗外天黑了，楼道的声控灯亮了一下，又灭了。

Winter 2023, Panyu, Guangzhou, a bidding room for a government project. The meeting room wasn't big, a round table, a few chairs, a whiteboard on the wall still marked with words from some other meeting that morning, nobody had wiped it. I sat on one side of the table, across from me sat a few representatives from the client side, and one of them, a middle-aged man, listened for a while and yawned without covering his mouth.

I held a deck in my hand, A4 paper, twenty-some pages. I was on page eight when he yawned. I stopped for two seconds, looked at him, he wasn't looking at me, he was looking out the window. Guangzhou was hot that day, the air conditioning was on in the room but it didn't reach my side, the small of my back was damp.

The study-tour trade had been grinding hard since 2022. I ran Penguin Exploration, and the first event was in 2023, out in Nansha, Guangzhou, on a farm, the kind they call an agricultural park. Dozens of families came that day, the farm had a vegetable plot, the kids squatted in the dirt picking greens, one small boy refused to stand up, fist full of bok choy he wanted to take home and eat. I stood at the edge of the plot watching, the sun wasn't too harsh, but squatting long enough made your legs go numb. After that event I thought this could work, the parents wanted to come, the kids wanted to squat down.

It didn't last.

The price comparisons in the parent groups got ruthless. One itinerary, five agencies quoting five prices, and parents would hash out a gap of a few dozen yuan for an entire afternoon. Someone stitched the five itineraries into a single image and dropped it in the group, comparing item by item, what time the meetup, what time the meal, whether insurance was included, what time the kids got dropped back. The parents compared it finer than procurement.

Route homogenization pushed in too. Of the outfits doing study tours in Guangzhou, four out of five ran the same intangible-heritage route, the same inheritor, the same workshop, and the handmade things the kids brought home were all the same. I went around and looked that year, came back and sat a long time at my desk in the co-working space, said nothing. The person at the desk across from me asked what was wrong. I said nothing, just thinking about how to be different from the rest.

Spring 2024, the event at the HKUST Guangzhou campus was an exception. I designed a route tied to campus research, walking the kids through the corridor outside a lab, letting them watch what the grad students were doing. That one hit a 60% conversion rate, with 40% organic traffic growth. But that was ground out event by event, and the whole time I was grinding it out, my palms were sweating.

The middle-aged man who yawned at the bidding that day ended up picking another agency. Their price was lower than mine, the route about the same. When I walked out of the meeting room, the sound-controlled light in the corridor came on, then went off. I stood there waiting for it to come on again, it didn't. I stamped my foot, it came on.

Juan, the word people use for the grind, put plainly it's everyone squeezing into the same spot. To break out of it you have to find the piece of ground nobody has squatted on yet. That kid at the farm down south, clutching his greens and refusing to leave, I remembered that moment a long time. Parents will pay for that instant of squatting down, they won't pay for an itinerary that looks like everyone else's.

Back home I opened an old file folder, the photo on the cover was the 2023 farm event, a few kids squatting in the field, backs to the camera, hands full of vegetables. Outside the window it had gone dark, the sound-controlled light in the hallway came on, then went off.
