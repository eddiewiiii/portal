长沙早高峰，2号线转6号线，单程四十分钟。我每天七点四十进站，车厢挤得转不开身，有人低头刷手机，有人闭着眼靠在车门上。我习惯站在车厢连接处，手扶着那根冰凉的杆子。这四十分钟一般放空，但偶尔脑子自己转起来，转到某个做决定的瞬间。

2022年初，还在易车。有天下午在广州TIT创意园的工位上，收到猎头消息，太平洋汽车在招人。我没马上回。易车是大厂，活儿顺，福利好，下午阳光从窗户斜进来照在键盘上。太平洋汽车也是大厂，但那个岗位接手的是一摊烂数据，进去得自己理。下班走到创意园门口，天阴着，风从那排旧厂房之间灌过来，吹得人清醒了一点。我掏出手机给猎头回了句“可以聊聊”。后来真去了。待了七个月。

七个月后决定出来创业。那天晚上在广州天河一个大排档，跟后来一起做企鹅探学的Flora吃饭。桌上是一盘炒花甲和两瓶啤酒，Flora把花甲壳吐在桌上，一边吐一边跟我算从零开始要花多少钱。我听着，筷子戳着碗里的饭。大排档的灯泡瓦数不够，照得人脸发黄。我说我可以辞。Flora看了我一眼，说你想好了。我说想好了。其实那天晚上没怎么想好，但回到出租屋躺在床上看天花板，觉得待在大厂里再待下去，人会钝掉。

2024年底，企鹅探学做了两年多。有天晚上在客厅改方案改到十一点，窗外广州的夜景还是那片灯。我关上电脑，在沙发上坐了一会儿。决定回长沙这件事没什么戏剧性的瞬间，就是在这个沙发上，忽然觉得广州这十年的灯，跟我没什么关系了。第二天跟Flora说了，她沉默了一会儿，说“你回去也好”。

回长沙之后白天上班，晚上偶尔去酒吧。酒吧叫葡滋，跟一个姓周的兄弟合伙开的。决定开酒吧那天是在他家里喝酒，喝到半夜，他说要不我们一起开个酒吧。我当时有点上头，说行。第二天醒来想了想，觉得也行。白天上班有白天的节奏，晚上酒吧有晚上的节奏，两件事搭在一起，人反而比只做一件事的时候清醒。

地铁报站，6号线到了。我松开扶着杆子的手，手心有点凉。车门开的时候，站台的风灌进来，带着一点地下的潮气。

Changsha morning rush hour. Line 2 to Line 6, forty minutes each way. I go through the gate at seven forty every morning. The car is packed too tight to turn around. Some people look down at their phones. Some lean against the door with their eyes closed. I like to stand in the section between cars, one hand on that cold metal pole. Most days I let my mind go blank for those forty minutes. But sometimes my brain starts up on its own, drifts to some moment when I made a decision.

Early 2022, still at Yiche. One afternoon at my desk in Guangzhou's TIT Creative Park, I got a message from a headhunter. Pacific Auto was hiring. I didn't reply right away. Yiche was a big company. The work flowed smoothly, the benefits were good, and in the afternoon the sun came through the window and fell across the keyboard. Pacific Auto was also a big company, but the role meant taking over a mess of data. You'd have to sort it out yourself. When I left work that day and walked to the gate of the creative park, the sky was overcast. Wind blew through the gaps between the old factory buildings and cleared my head a little. I pulled out my phone and sent the headhunter: “Sure, let's talk.” I went. Stayed seven months.

Seven months later I decided to leave and start something. That night at a dai pai dong in Tianhe, Guangzhou, I had dinner with Flora, who would later start Penguin Exploration with me. On the table: a plate of stir-fried clams, two bottles of beer. Flora spat the clam shells onto the table, counting out loud how much it would cost to start from zero. I listened, poking at the rice in my bowl with my chopsticks. The light bulb at the dai pai dong wasn't bright enough. It made everyone's face look yellow. I said I could quit. Flora looked at me and said, you sure about this? I said I was sure. Truth is I wasn't really sure that night. But back in my apartment, lying on the bed staring at the ceiling, I felt like staying in a big company any longer would dull me.

Late 2024, Penguin Exploration had been going for over two years. One night I was revising a proposal in the living room until eleven. Outside, the Guangzhou skyline was the same stretch of lights as always. I closed the laptop and sat on the couch for a while. Deciding to go back to Changsha didn't have a dramatic moment. It was on that couch, suddenly feeling like the last ten years of Guangzhou's lights had nothing to do with me. I told Flora the next day. She was quiet for a moment, then said, “It's good for you to go back.”

After moving back to Changsha, I work during the day and sometimes go to the bar at night. The bar is called Puzi, started with a partner whose family name is Zhou. The night we decided to open it, I was at his place drinking. Past midnight, he said, let's open a bar together. I was a bit drunk. I said sure. The next morning I thought about it, and it still seemed fine. The day job has its own rhythm, the bar has its own rhythm. The two of them together, somehow I feel more clear-headed than when I was only doing one thing.

The subway announced the station. Line 6. I let go of the pole. My palm was cold. When the doors opened, air from the platform rushed in, carrying a faint dampness from underground.
