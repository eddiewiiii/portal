入职第一天，老板给了我一个任务：一个月内，把"晓A精灵"这个AI英语教培品牌的整套体系做出来。

定位、视觉、手册、官网、发布会，全要。

我当时坐在工位上，盯着这句话看了很久。三十天完成品牌定位、视觉体系、官网、发布会。时间短，人手少，要求高。我当时心想，好家伙。

第三天晚上，我对着一堆竞品资料和用户反馈，卡住了。

过去两天我一直在想"我们要做什么"，AI教学、智能辅导、个性化学习，方向列了一堆，每个看起来都对，但每个也都模糊。那天晚上我把所有方向写在白板上，盯着看了半个小时，突然意识到问题出在哪：我一直在加东西，没有减。

第二天我换了个思路，不去想"做什么"，去想"不做什么"。

我和创始人连续聊了一周，每天一小时，把过去积累的用户反馈、竞品资料全部过了一遍。聊到第四天的时候，他说了一句话，"其实家长选我们，不是因为我们AI教得好，是因为孩子来了之后开始主动说英语了。"

这句话一下点醒了我。这个品牌的核心不是"AI教学"，是"让孩子爱上学习"。这个认知差，就是品牌定位的起点。最终我们的定位是：精准发音，全球对话，你的AI英语专家。slogan是"发音要准，进步要狠，未来要稳"。

定位定了之后，剩下的事情反而快了。

品牌手册我做了两版。一版完整版，给设计团队和外部供应商用，该有的都有。另一版精简版，给销售、市场、运营看，只有10页，每页都在回答同一个问题："我该怎么用这套品牌？"很多品牌手册做完后除了设计师没人看得懂，我觉得那是浪费资源。

官网只做了三个页面：首页、产品介绍、品牌故事。交互逻辑我自己做原型，设计只负责视觉执行。不追求完美，追求"能上线、能看懂"。品牌专属的九阶成长体系和对应的学习要求也定了下来。

发布会全案是我自己写的，从创意策划到视觉系统，再到创始人的核心演讲模块。核心就一件事：把"品牌是什么"讲清楚，把"优势是什么"说明白。不堆PPT。

三十天后我们上线了。品牌体系不算完美，但它跑起来了。整个团队从"不知道怎么介绍自己"变成了"每个人都能说清楚我们是谁"。

发布会结束那晚，我一个人坐在办公室里。灯关了大半，只有工位上的台灯还亮着。桌面上散着白板笔、品牌手册的打印稿、一杯凉了的咖啡。

累吗？累。但那三十天其实是在帮我自己理清一件事：一个品牌的核心，不是logo，不是颜色，是一套完整的认知体系。知道什么该做，什么不该做。这个道理我当时说不清楚，但现在回头看，那三十天里每一个卡壳的时刻，都在替我回答这个问题。

My first day on the job, the boss gave me a task: build the entire brand system for "XiaoA Jingling," an AI English tutoring brand, in one month.

Positioning, visual identity, brand guidelines, website, launch event. Everything.

I sat at my desk staring at that sentence for a long time. Thirty days for brand positioning, visual system, website, and a launch event. Tight timeline, small team, high bar. Okay then, I thought.

On the third night, I got stuck.

I'd spent two days thinking about "what we should do",AI teaching, smart tutoring, personalized learning. I listed a bunch of directions. Each one sounded right, but each one was also vague. That night I wrote them all on the whiteboard and stared at them for half an hour. Then it hit me: I'd been adding things the whole time, never subtracting.

The next day I flipped the question. Instead of "what should we do," I asked "what should we not do."

I talked with the founder for a straight week, an hour a day, going through all the accumulated user feedback and competitor materials. On the fourth day, he said something,"Actually, parents don't choose us because our AI teaching is good. They choose us because after coming here, their kids start speaking English on their own."

That woke me up. The core of this brand wasn't "AI teaching." It was "making kids fall in love with learning." That gap in perception was the starting point for the brand positioning. We landed on: precise pronunciation, global conversation, your AI English expert. Slogan: "Pin yin yao zhun, jin bu yao hen, wei lai yao wen",accurate pronunciation, fierce progress, steady future.

Once the positioning was set, everything else moved fast.

I made two versions of the brand guidelines. One was the full version, for the design team and external vendors,everything in there. The other was a slim version for sales, marketing, and operations,ten pages, each answering the same question: "How do I use this brand?" Most brand guidelines are incomprehensible to anyone besides the designer. That's a waste.

The website was three pages: home, product, brand story. I did the interaction prototypes myself; design handled visuals. Not chasing perfect,chasing "shippable and readable." The brand's proprietary nine-level growth system and its learning requirements got locked in too.

I wrote the entire launch event plan myself,creative concept, visual system, the founder's keynote. The core was simple: explain what the brand is, explain what the advantage is. No slide-stuffing.

Thirty days later, we launched. The brand system wasn't perfect, but it ran. The whole team went from "not knowing how to introduce ourselves" to "everyone can explain who we are."

The night after the launch event, I sat alone in the office. Most of the lights were off,just my desk lamp. Scattered on the desk: whiteboard markers, a printout of the brand guidelines, a cup of cold coffee.

Was I tired? Yes. But those thirty days were really helping me figure out one thing: the core of a brand isn't the logo or the colors. It's a complete cognitive system,knowing what to do and what not to do. I couldn't have articulated that at the time, but looking back, every moment I got stuck during those thirty days was answering that question for me.
