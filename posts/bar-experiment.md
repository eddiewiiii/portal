装修第八十三天，长沙下着雨。我蹲在毛坯房里盯着报价单，水管走线多了一千八，灯光返工加三千，吧台那块老榆木比预算贵了将近一倍。合伙人老周在旁边抽烟，一脚踩灭烟头。我俩都没说话。那是2025年9月，距开业还有三个月。

我在广州做了十年品牌，2025年回长沙。白天上班，晚上去酒吧坐着，慢慢想自己开一个。葡滋，2025年12月在长沙河西开的，我是联合创始人。联合两个字，意思就是拍板的是老周，他出钱我出脑子。

选铺面跑了十几个地方，五一广场房租能把人吃掉，梅溪湖太新，洋湖太远。最后定在保利山姆楼上，楼下是山姆会员店，楼上是商业空间。那天是周末，山姆门口排队排到了马路边，购物车挤成一团。老周站在楼上往下看，说这个流量能沾上一点就行。我也觉得能沾上。这个判断后来被证明是我整件事里最大的一个错，但那天我们俩都很兴奋。

办证是另一座山。每张纸背后都是一个窗口，我在政务中心排了一天，办完两个章。柜台后面的大姐问我，你们这种小酒吧能活过一年吗。我说试试看。我做了十年品牌，那天发现品牌两个字在柜台前一个都用不上，人家不认PPT。

定酒单是我最熟的部分。左边经典款八款，右边十款我起名“城市灵感系列”。头一版写太满，调酒师阿杰看完说，哥，客人喝酒不想读作文。删到每款只剩一句话。“暗恋桃子”，藏在白桃香气里的青涩心动。“半醉长沙”，献给星城打工人的体面微醺。阿杰试了三版才把“湘女多情”调对，朗姆配椰奶，多了腻少了薄。

阿杰后来走了，女朋友要去深圳，跟着走的。临走前一晚我们在吧台喝完最后一杯“冷萃夜色”，他说这杯酒是给白天上班晚上做梦的人准备的，他也准备换个地方做梦。我没留他。开酒吧这种事，我后来才发现人比钱散得早。

新调酒师叫小唐，二十三岁，手稳但不懂长沙。我带他去岳麓山走了一圈，五月清晨有薄雾，草木是潮的。他回来调出“五月岳麓山”，灰蒙蒙的紫。“爱晚亭的晚风”也是他调的，他尝了一口说，这风是凉的。我没告诉他我当时也想这么说。

菜单是另一摊事。老周想做纯西餐，我说长沙人半夜不会想吃牛排。最后定了融合路线，暹罗酸辣脆皮鸡是招牌，湘西的山胡椒麻辣牛肉条一上桌木姜子油的味道满屋子都是。

开业那天是2025年12月12号。晚上八点店里坐了三桌，我站在吧台后面切柠檬，手一直在抖。十一点吧台满了。有个女孩点了一杯“暗恋桃子”，喝完问我这酒有故事吗。我说有，是没说出口的那种。她笑了一下，说我也有一段。那晚收摊凌晨两点，流水不到三千，老周算了半天没说话。

没有往上走。开业第二周开始，工作日的晚上经常只有两三桌。楼下山姆的人流确实大，可他们推着满满的购物车出来，直奔停车场，没有人抬头看一眼楼上还有个酒吧。有次我站在窗前往下看，一个拎着两箱牛奶的大哥走出来，连脚步都没慢。老周说也许周末会好。多几桌，但都是美团搜来喝完就走的。我原来以为山姆的流量能自然溢到楼上来，开业以后才知道，山姆的人和酒吧的人不是同一拨。

算到现在，葡滋开了七个月。老周还在算账，小唐还在调酒。坐2号线转6号线去店里，五十分钟够过一遍备货。上个月有个客人连来四天，每天同一杯“爱晚亭的晚风”，第四天问酒名字谁起的。我说我。他说，挺好的。

那天晚上他走以后，店里就空了。小唐在擦吧台，空调嗡嗡响。我坐在吧台最里面，面前一杯没喝完的水。楼下山姆的灯还亮着，停车场还有车在往外走。

On the eighty-third day of renovation, it was raining in Changsha. I squatted in the bare concrete room staring at the quotation. Plumbing rerun, eighteen hundred more. Lighting redesign, three thousand extra. The old elm slab for the bar was nearly double the budget. My partner Lao Zhou stood beside me smoking, grinding the cigarette out with his shoe. Neither spoke. That was September 2025, three months before opening.

I had spent ten years in brand work in Guangzhou before moving back to Changsha in 2025. I worked a day job, sat in bars at night, and started wanting one of my own. Puzi opened in December 2025 on the west bank of the river. I am the co-founder. The word co means Lao Zhou calls the shots. His money, my ideas.

We scouted a dozen spots and settled on the floor above Sam's Club in the Poly mall. Downstairs is the membership store, upstairs is commercial space. The day we went to look it was a weekend. The line at Sam's spilled out to the curb, shopping carts clanking together. Lao Zhou stood upstairs looking down and said, if we catch even a fraction of this foot traffic we are fine. I thought so too. That judgment turned out to be the biggest mistake of the whole project, but that day we were both excited.

The permits were another mountain. Each piece of paper had a window behind it. I lined up at the government center all day and finished two stamps. The woman behind the counter asked whether a small bar like ours could survive a year. I said we will see. Ten years of brand work, and that day I learned the word brand was useless at a government counter.

The drink list was the part I knew best. Eight classics on the left, the usual. The ten on the right I named the City Inspiration Series. The first draft was bloated. The bartender A-Jie read it and said, brother, people who are drinking do not want to read essays. We cut until each drink had one line. Peach Secret, the green crush hidden in white peach aroma. Half-Drunk, a decent buzz for the Star City wage earner. A-Jie took three tries to get Miss Tiffany right, rum with coconut milk.

A-Jie left later. His girlfriend was moving to Shenzhen and he went with her. The night before he left we finished the last glass of Espresso Dream at the bar. He said that drink was for people who work by day and dream by night, and he was going to dream somewhere else. I did not ask him to stay. In this business, people scatter before money does.

The new bartender is Xiao Tang, twenty-three, steady hands, but he did not know Changsha. I took him to walk Yuelu Mountain. A May morning with thin mist, the grass and trees damp. He came back and mixed Yuelu Mountain, that grayish purple. AiWan Breeze was his too. He took a sip and said, this wind is cool. I did not tell him I had been about to say the same thing.

The menu was another mess. Lao Zhou wanted pure Western food. I said people in Changsha do not crave steak at midnight. We ended up with a fusion menu. The Siamese sour-spicy crispy chicken is the signature. The western Hunan mountain pepper beef jerky fills the whole room with mujiangzi oil the moment it hits the table.

Opening day was December 12, 2025. At eight in the evening three tables were seated. I stood behind the bar slicing lemons, hands shaking. By eleven the bar was full. A girl ordered Peach Secret, finished it, and asked if the drink had a story. I said it did, the kind you never said out loud. She smiled and said she had one too. We closed at two in the morning. Revenue was under three thousand yuan. Lao Zhou worked the costs for a long time, said nothing.

They did not go up. From the second week on, weeknights often meant two or three tables. The foot traffic at Sam's downstairs was real. But those people pushed their full shopping carts out the door and headed straight for the parking lot. Nobody looked up. One time I watched a man walk out carrying two crates of milk. He never broke stride. Lao Zhou said maybe weekends would be better. Weekends were slightly better, but most found us on Dianping and left after one drink. I had assumed Sam's foot traffic would naturally spill upstairs. After we opened I learned that Sam's shoppers and bar people are simply not the same crowd.

Counting to now, Puzi has been open seven months. Lao Zhou still does the books. Xiao Tang still mixes drinks. Line 2 to Line 6, fifty minutes to the shop. Last month a guest came four days in a row, ordering the same AiWan Breeze. On the fourth day he asked who named the drinks. I said, me. He said, nice.

After he left that night the shop went quiet. Xiao Tang wiped down the bar. The air conditioning hummed. I sat in the innermost chair with a half-finished glass of water. Downstairs, the lights at Sam's were still on. Cars were still pulling out of the parking lot.
